title: What 15 years in tech taught me about patience
date: 2026-02-05
tag: self
featured: true
---

The thing nobody tells you about a long career in tech is how much of it is waiting.

Waiting for the data to come in. Waiting for engineering to be unblocked. Waiting for the org to be ready for an idea that was right two years ago. Waiting for your own thinking to catch up with your instincts.

I've shipped products that took three years from first conversation to launch. I've had ideas that felt obviously right and got killed, then watched someone else ship the same thing at another company and succeed. I've seen careers accelerate and stall and recover in ways that made no sense in the moment.

What patience has taught me: most good things take longer than you think they should, and the waiting is usually where the real work happens.

The conviction you build while you're waiting — that's what makes the product good when you finally ship it.
